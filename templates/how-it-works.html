How it Works

1. Document Parsing

We accept .srt subtitle files and plain .txt scripts. Our pipeline extracts spoken dialogue and scene cues, stripping out away timestamps and HTML tags using the pysrt library, giving us a clean corpus of text to analyze seamlessly.

2. Named Entity Recognition (NER)

The text is fed into a custom spaCy NLP pipeline that has been specifically fine-tuned and populated with custom matchers to identify key compliance entities: Profanity, Violence, Gore, Drugs, and Nudity descriptors. The frequency of these terms directly informs the final rating system.

3. Region-Specific Compliance Check

Using CBFC-style guidelines from around the world (India, USA, UK, Australia, etc.), our rules engine evaluates the entity counts. A high score of explicit language might trigger an "R" rating in the US but a "15" in the UK. The global map translates raw entity data into accurate local certifications automatically.
